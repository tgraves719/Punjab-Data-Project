# Prior-hand marginalia — 1910Q1 verso (doc[52])

The final leaf of the 1910Q1 catalogue (PDF `doc[52]`, the blank verso following printed page 51) carries a **handwritten index in an earlier, unidentified hand**. This annotation **predates Prof. Davis's acquisition of the volumes** — confirmed with him directly (July 2026): the marks were already on the leaves when he obtained them. Provenance is therefore unknown; the annotator was plausibly a prior owner, librarian, or scholar-reader who worked through these bound catalogues before they reached Davis's collection.

It is not part of the imperial record; it is a reader's working finding-aid, listing the **multi-part / serialized works** in this quarter to track across installments. Entries are keyed **`page.serial`** (printed page number, then the serial-number-in-section) and tagged with a **language code**.

The same-style verso index recurs in the other 1910 quarters (see `../1910Q3/marginalia_p058.md`, `../1910Q4/marginalia_p038.md`) — evidently one continuous hand across the run. It is captured here (not in the verbatim entry records) because it is a **second-order, later-hand annotation of the archive** — precisely the "observation operator" layer the project models, and now doubly interesting: an unknown reader's pass over the imperial catalogue, layered between the register and us. The referenced entries themselves are transcribed normally in the per-page extraction JSONs; the `marks` field on those entries notes the in-line pencil X / "Specimen" / "not available" marks, while THIS file documents the consolidated verso index.

## Language codes
- **H.** = Hindi (Devanagari)
- **U.** = Urdu (Perso-Arabic)
- **Punj.** = Punjabi (Gurmukhi)
- **S.-H.** = Sanskrit–Hindi (bilingual Sanskrit text + Hindi)

## Notes
- The top four items (p.4, p.6, p.7×2) point to the **front pages of Q1 (printed pp. 4–7)**, in the SIKH-RELIGION / HINDI / PUNJABI opening sections (see p004–p007 JSONs); the annotator was tracking their multi-volume continuations.
- The bottom four items are the **serialized-scripture and multi-part biographical works** on the later pages (Ali biographies, Kshatriya-hero biography, and the two great Arya-Samaj scripture serials — the Gita/Upanishad "Arsh Granthawali" and the Rig Veda Samhita).
- The **S.-H. (Sanskrit–Hindi) tag** on the two Arya-Samaj serials is the annotator's shorthand for the bilingual scripture format; these are the same serials whose "December 1909" back-issue cross-references produced the earlier OCR false-positive of a phantom 1909 supplement.
- The separate in-line marks on Q1 entries — pencil **X** (very frequent, esp. down the URDU—BIOGRAPHY and FICTION columns), **"Specimen"** (on the periodical entries: Punjabi Bhain p50, Anath Hiteshi p51, Vedic Philosophy p51), and **"not available"** (e.g. Chandraoli, p26 s1) — are recorded in the `marks` field of the individual extraction records, not here. Their meaning is likewise the prior annotator's, and remains a provenance question rather than a claim about Davis's collection.
