# Handwritten marginalia — 1910Q3, final verso (printed p. 58 / pdf 154)

The verso of the September 1910 quarter's last leaf carries no printed entries.
It is covered in cursive pencil notes — an earlier reader's finding-aid (the same
hand as the X-marks and pencil numerals through the quarter). This annotation
**predates Prof. Davis's acquisition of the volumes** (confirmed with him, July
2026); the annotator is unidentified — a prior owner, librarian, or scholar-reader.
It indexes **multi-part / serialized works** in this catalog, keyed `page.serial`,
with language codes:

Interpretation: the annotator was tracking **works issued in parts** (to watch for
continuations in other quarters) — consistent with a collector/bibliographer
assembling complete sets. Since the hand predates Davis, this is a **provenance
question about a prior owner/reader**, not about Davis's own collection. The
apparatus travels with the X-marks and the running pencil numerals (which reach
~116 by page 57 and appear to count selected items).
